3 powerful reasons to start strength training

1

Build long-term health and longevity

Strength is the foundation for an active, independent life. Regular strength training helps maintain muscle mass, increase bone density, improve metabolic health, and support healthy ageing.

2

Improve performance and prevent injury

A stronger body is a more resilient body. Strength training enhances athletic performance, improves balance and coordination, and reduces the risk of injury by strengthening muscles, joints, and connective tissues.

3

Boost confidence and mental wellbeing

Progress in the gym builds more than physical strength. Setting goals, overcoming challenges, and seeing measurable improvements can increase confidence, reduce stress, and improve overall mental wellbeing.

FAQ

Is strength training suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. Every program starts with an assessment and is built around your current ability. We teach correct technique from day one and progress you at a pace that’s safe and sustainable – no experience required.

Is there a lock-in contract?

No lock-in contracts. Our coaching is designed to fit your life, and you’re free to adjust or pause your sessions as your schedule and goals change.

Can I train if I have an injury or illness?

In most cases, yes. Strength training is one of the best tools for rehabilitation and resilience. We tailor every session around your history and, where needed, work alongside your health professionals.

Is parking available?

Yes – there is convenient parking close to the studio in Five Dock, so getting to your session is quick and easy.

What age groups do you train?

We coach people of all ages – from aspiring athletes and busy professionals to active retirees. Programs are always personalised to suit your age, ability and goals.

Every rep builds resilience.

Adults lose approximately 3–8% of muscle mass per decade after 30 if they don’t regularly strength train. After age 60, muscle loss often speeds up.
